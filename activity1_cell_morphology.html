Cell morphology

Three common prokaryotic cell shapes

Most introductory examples of prokaryotic cell morphology fall into three broad categories:

  1. Coccus — spherical
  2. Bacillus — rod-shaped
  3. Spirillum — spiral-shaped

Many bacterial names hint at shape. For example, Streptococcus includes the root coccus, which points to a spherical form.

Quick check

Which term describes a rod-shaped bacterial cell?

Answer: Bacillus.

Which term describes a spiral-shaped bacterial cell?

Answer: Spirillum.
