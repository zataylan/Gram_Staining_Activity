Introduction

What this mini-site covers

You will review bacterial cell structure, learn the main prokaryotic cell shapes, and work through the logic behind Gram staining.

Begin the content

Recommended sequence

  1. Read the bacteria overview.
  2. Watch the short review video.
  3. Study the three common prokaryotic cell shapes.
  4. Review how Gram staining separates bacteria into two broad groups.
  5. Walk through the stain steps in order.

The original Canvas export mixed in unrelated course directions and assignment deadlines. Those were removed because they did not belong on a public mini-site.

What to focus on

  • The difference between prokaryotic and eukaryotic cells
  • The meaning of coccus, bacillus, and spirillum
  • Why Gram-positive cells stay purple
  • Why Gram-negative cells turn pink after counterstaining
